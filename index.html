DGX Spark Workstation Guide

Purpose

The NVIDIA DGX Spark is a high-performance workstation designed for extensive AI and data science workloads. It uses 128 GB of unified memory architecture which enables training and inferencing large machine learning models. Two may be connected together to create a 256 GB system.

Getting Started

Email me at garciaml@uw.edu to request access to this workstation. I will need your SSH public key to enable login.

Generate an SSH key pair:

ssh-keygen -t rsa -b 4096 -C "UNetID@uw.edu"

Send your public key (from ~/.ssh/id_rsa.pub) with your UNetID. I will reply with the assigned workstation. Then log in using: 

ssh [UNetID]@[workstation].meb120.me.washington.edu

If you are not on the UW network, you will need to connect via Husky OnNet VPN first: https://itconnect.uw.edu/connect/remote-access/vpn/husky-onnet-vpn/

JupyterLab

JupyterLab is installed on each DGX Spark workstation. After SSH login, check your assigned Jupyter port: 

cat /opt/nvidia/dgx-dashboard-service/jupyterlab_ports.yaml

Find your UNetID and assigned port, then open an SSH tunnel: 

ssh -L 11000:localhost:11000 -L [PORT]:localhost:[PORT] [UNetID]@[workstation].meb120.me.washington.edu

Open http://localhost:11000 in your browser and click Start, then click Open in Browser to launch the notebook.

To close your session: close the terminal running the tunnel or press Ctrl + C.
